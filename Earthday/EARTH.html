Welcome

Earth Day is celebrated annually on April 22 to raise awareness about environmental protection and sustainability. This website was created to educate people about environmental challenges and encourage everyone to take action for a healthier planet and better future.

About Earth Day

Earth Day, founded by Senator Gaylord Nelson, began in 1970, as a global movement focused on protecting the environment. The aim was to raise awareness on increasing amount of pollution, deforestation and climate change. It was held on the 22nd of April to maximize student participation and ensure a good weather for outdoor events. Millions of people around the world participate in activities such as tree planting, recycling campaigns, and clean-up events. This eventually led to the creation of the Environmental Protection Agency.


Today, Earth Day highlights major environmental challenges including climate change, pollution, deforestation, and biodiversity loss. The goal is to inspire people, communities, and governments to work together to protect the Earth.

Environmental Issues

Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperature and weather patterns caused mainly by human activities.


Causes:
  • Burning fossil fuels
  • Industrial pollution
  • Deforestation
  • Agriculture and live stock

Effects:
  • Extreme weather conditions
  • Rising sea levels
  • Damage to ecosystems

Water Pollution

Water pollution occurs when harmful substances contaminate rivers, lakes, and oceans, affecting both humans and wildlife.


Causes:
  • Plastic waste
  • Chemical dumping
  • Oil spills
  • Agricultural runoffs

Effects:
  • Unsafe drinking water
  • Death of marine life
  • Spread of diseases

Deforestation

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of forests, often for agriculture or urban development.


Causes:
  • Logging
  • Farming expansion
  • Urbanization

Effects:
  • Loss of wildlife habitats
  • Increased carbon emissions
  • Soil erosion

Take Action

Small actions can make a huge difference. Here are practical ways to live more sustainably:

  • 🌱 Plant trees and protect green spaces
  • ♻ Reduce, reuse, and recycle waste
  • 🚲 Use public transport, walk, or cycle
  • 💧 Save water and electricity
  • 🛍 Avoid single-use plastics
  • 🌍 Join local environmental campaigns and clean-up events
